Teaching & Outreach

As much as I enjoy scientific research, I am also passionate about teaching both in and out of the classroom. My teaching philosophy is to prioritize building curiosity, developing skills, and fostering scientific thinking. I focus on big-picture concepts, transferrable skills, and thinking through examples, rather than on recalling any given piece of information, viewing much of the factual content as a means to these ends and as an opportunity to engage student interest.

I was an NIH IRACDA postdoctoral fellow with the Emory FIRST program until its recent termination. As part of the fellowship, I co-taught Animal Behavior at Spelman College and developed a syllabus for Behavioral Endocrinology. I have also taught human evolution as instructor of record, as well as multiple classes as a teaching assistant (see CV for details). If you are teaching similar courses and are interested in discussing or sharing materials, please be in touch!

I also strive to bring science into K-12 classrooms and to the public, in order to increase accessibility to science and scientists.
